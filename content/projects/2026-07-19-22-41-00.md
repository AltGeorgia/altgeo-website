title: The COMPASS-GE project ("Comprehensive Approach to Integrated and
  Accessible Mental Health in Georgia"), officially launched under the
  leadership of Global Initiative on Psychiatry-Tbilisi (GIP-T), is a four-year
  initiative designed to comprehensively strengthen Georgia's mental healthcare
  system through rights-based, evidence-informed policies and practices. Funded
  by the European Commission, the project's overall goal is to modernize
  national mental health legislation, enhance stakeholder capacities, build
  public literacy, and systematically eliminate coercive institutional practices
  in favor of care models that respect human dignity. As a vital consortium
  partner, Alternative Georgia leads the project's Addiction Integration
  Cluster, managing a dedicated two-year component aimed at bridging the
  historical gap between the addiction and mental health fields. AltGeorgia is
  specifically responsible for conducting comprehensive situation and needs
  assessments, collaboratively developing an strategic integration Action Plan
  aligned with national strategies, and building institutional capacity through
  accredited workshops. Their work focuses on developing specialized toolkits,
  treatment protocols, and ongoing clinical mentorship to significantly enhance
  the capability of healthcare personnel to accurately diagnose and provide
  integrated care for individuals with co-occurring mental health and substance
  use disorders (dual diagnosis).
