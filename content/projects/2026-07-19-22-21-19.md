description: >-
  This comprehensive initiative aims to increase human security and safety in
  Georgia by fostering a human rights-based drug policy and enhancing the
  country’s crime and drug prevention, treatment, and care systems. Operating
  across six distinct yet interlinked specific objectives, the project
  implements a logical progression of interventions that begin with needs
  assessments and regulatory framework updates - including new service
  standards, delivery guidelines, and clinical protocols. These steps are
  systematically followed by evidence-based professional education, public
  perception surveys, and strategic networking. The action spans multiple
  sectors to build cross-cutting institutional capacity: it trains law
  enforcement and educational personnel to manage violent behaviors; expands
  healthcare capacity to deliver integrated mental health and youth substance
  use disorder (SUD) services; introduces international prevention curricula in
  schools, media, and nightlife facilities; reframes national drug legislation
  via public advocacy and legal support; and directly strengthens local civil
  society organizations (CSOs) through targeted sub-grants and organizational
  capacity building. 

  AltGeorgia's Role AltGeorgia was directly responsible for Specific Objective 4 (SO4), leading the efforts to build the capacity of the healthcare system to timely identify, engage, and provide client-centered, effective treatment and care. In this vital role, AltGeorgia focused on introducing integrated care approaches and ensuring a continuum of care for individuals with substance use disorders. To achieve this, the organization was tasked with strengthening the primary healthcare system through the introduction of age-sensitive clinical protocols and educational programs. Furthermore, AltGeorgia worked to enhance collaboration between primary healthcare and specialized SUD services, develop updated clinical guidelines and online training modules, and introduce standardized patient assessment and monitoring tools. These targeted activities directly addressed critical knowledge and skill gaps among professionals working with people who use drugs (PWUD), including vulnerable children and youth experimenting with or at risk of drug use.
