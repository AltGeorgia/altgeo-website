description: The Georgia Medical Cannabis Project (GMCP), implemented by
  Alternative Georgia with the support of the Open Society Georgia Foundation
  (OSGF), is a 12-month public health and advocacy initiative designed to
  support the introduction of medical cannabis into Georgia’s healthcare system
  and establish a sustainable framework for multi-purpose cannabis cultivation.
  The project aims to benefit approximately 30,000 patients suffering from
  conditions such as chronic pain, anxiety, PTSD, seizures, and
  chemotherapy-related nausea, while simultaneously opening up economic
  opportunities for local farmers and entrepreneurs in the industrial and
  cosmetic sectors. To build a supportive environment for these reforms, the
  initiative focuses on lobbying the Parliament, analyzing legal barriers, and
  conducting surveys to examine public and professional attitudes. Furthermore,
  the project utilizes workshops, webinars, and international trials to educate
  healthcare workers, alongside conducting crucial macroeconomic assessments and
  developing legal safeguard measures to protect local stakeholders from unfair
  corporate monopolization in the emerging market.
