description: The PRIDE (Prison-related Research, Intervention Development and
  Evaluation) project—specifically its third generation, PRIDE 3 - is a
  multi-national implementation science network focused on scaling up opioid
  agonist therapies (OAT) as a primary method of HIV prevention and treatment
  within criminal justice settings across Eastern Europe and Central Asia
  (EECA). Operating in countries with some of the highest incarceration rates
  globally, the project expands its collaborative scope to include new
  institutional partners in Georgia (Alternative Georgia) and Tajikistan,
  alongside established networks in Ukraine, Moldova, and Kyrgyzstan. The
  project relies on the EPIS (Explore, Prepare, Implement, Sustain) framework
  paired with the evidence-informed NIATx process improvement model to deploy
  tailored interventions across closed environments, pretrial detention centers
  (SIZO), and newly formed probation systems. To overcome entrenched societal,
  inter-professional, and institutional barriers against OAT, the initiative
  employs rapid-cycle Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) changes and Project ECHO
  tele-education loops to effectively cross-train non-specialty prison and
  probation personnel. By mapping treatment workflows, improving entry
  efficiencies, optimizing medication dosages, and securing transition
  continuums back into community-based settings upon release, the project seeks
  to align public safety and public health mandates while mitigating the
  volatile syndemics of injection drug use, OUD, and HIV in the region.
